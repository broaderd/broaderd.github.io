About CV Notes

Hello 👋

My name is Dillon, and I am an alumnus of the School of Physics at Trinity College Dublin. Trinity, founded in 1592, is Ireland's oldest and top-ranked university. The School of Physics recently celebrated its 300th anniversary at the institution. Its most notable alumnus, Ernest Walton, shared the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1951 with John Cockcroft of Cambridge University "for their pioneering work on the transmutation of atomic nuclei by artificially accelerated atomic particles."

I recently graduated with a Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Quantum Science and Technology. The program focused on the theoretical and software aspects of quantum technology, offering a unique blend of quantum information science, the physics of quantum hardware, and quantum materials. As far as I know, it is the only program of its kind in Ireland.

Specifically, I completed the following modules:

  • Introduction to Quantum Science
  • Open Quantum Systems
  • Physical Implementation of Quantum Technology
  • Quantum Computation and Algorithms
  • Quantum Material Science
  • Special Topics and the Quantum Industry

More information on the M.Sc. program can be found here.

I also conducted a 12-week research project in the Quantum Information Theory Group at Trinity. The title of my dissertation was Petz Recovery for Quantum Processes with Memory. My supervisors were Felix Binder and Simon Milz.

Prior to the masters, I completed my undergraduate in Physics at Trinity. This was a standard 4-year physics degree with a strong emphasis on Condensed Matter Physics and Electromagnetism. I undertook a 9-week research project in the Computational Spintronics Group at Trinity under the supervision of Stefano Sanvito. The title of my dissertation was Calculating the Magnetic Critical Temperature from Experimental Data. In this project, I simulated the effect of disorder on the magnetic critical temperature of an iron lattice.
